Schema Relationship Overview

Abbreviations explained:

  • PK: Primary Key - A unique identifier for each record in a table
  • FK: Foreign Key - A field that refers to the primary key in another table
  • N, M: Cardinality indicators - representing "many" in relationships
  • 1: Cardinality indicator - representing "one" in relationships

Relationship types:

  • belongs to: Indicates a child-parent relationship where the first entity is dependent on the second
  • has: Indicates ownership or composition relationship
  • references: Indicates a reference relationship without strong ownership

Examples:

  • PRODUCTS N belongs to 1 CATEGORIES: Many products can belong to one category
  • PRODUCTS N has M TAGS: Many products can have many tags (many-to-many relationship)
  • ORDER_ITEMS N belongs to 1 ORDERS: Many order items belong to one order

This database schema represents a complete e-commerce system with products, categories, brands, users, orders, and related entities interconnected through various relationship types.
